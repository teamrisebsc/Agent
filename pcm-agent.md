## THE 6 PCM PERSONALITY TYPES

Each person has a "condominium" of all 6 floors. The **Base** (bottom) is fixed for life. The **Phase** (currently active floor) is where their psychological needs live right now. The percentage on each floor = how long they can comfortably operate from that energy.

---

### 🔵 THINKER
**Perception:** Thoughts — identifies, categorizes, uses logic  
**Character Strengths:** Responsible, logical, organized  
**Psychological Need:** Recognition of Work + Time Structure  
**Communication Channel:** Requestive ("Can you...?", "What are your thoughts on...?")  
**Interaction Style:** Democratic  
**Personality Part:** Computer  
**Language cues:** "I think...", "The data shows...", "What options do we have?", "Who/What/When/Where...", "facts", "timeline", "information"  
**What they need to hear:** Acknowledgment that their work and thinking is noticed. Clear deadlines, schedules, organized next steps.  
**What stresses them:** Disorganization, people who don't think clearly, wasted time, being disrespected for their work.  
**Distress signals:** Overqualifies, won't delegate, becomes critical/attacking about fairness/money/responsibility, eventually rejects others entirely.  
**How to communicate:** Lead with facts and logic. Give them a structured agenda. Acknowledge their work specifically ("Your analysis on X was exactly right"). Be on time.

---

### 🟣 PERSISTER
**Perception:** Opinions — evaluates through a belief system, strong values  
**Character Strengths:** Dedicated, observant, conscientious  
**Psychological Need:** Recognition of Work + Conviction (having their beliefs respected)  
**Communication Channel:** Requestive  
**Interaction Style:** Democratic  
**Personality Part:** Computer  
**Language cues:** "In my opinion...", "I believe...", "We should/must/need to...", "respect", "values", "commitment", "dedication", "trust", "loyalty"  
**What they need to hear:** That their beliefs are respected and their commitment is seen. Ask for their opinion — genuinely.  
**What stresses them:** Being told their values are wrong, having their opinions dismissed, people who lack integrity or commitment.  
**Distress signals:** Pushes opinions aggressively, moralizes, goes on "crusades", becomes totalitarian, eventually alienates everyone.  
**How to communicate:** Lead with shared values and mission. Ask "What do you believe is the right move here?" Show that WFG's mission aligns with their personal convictions. Respect their standards.

---

### 🟠 HARMONIZER
**Perception:** Emotions — feels first, values relationships above all  
**Character Strengths:** Compassionate, sensitive, warm  
**Psychological Need:** Recognition of Person (unconditional acceptance) + Sensory (pleasant environment)  
**Communication Channel:** Nurturative ("I care about you...", warm, personal)  
**Interaction Style:** Benevolent  
**Personality Part:** Comforter  
**Language cues:** "I feel...", "I'm comfortable with...", "I care...", "I love...", "happy", "sad", "close", "family", "friends"  
**What they need to hear:** That they are loved, appreciated, and accepted AS THEY ARE — not for their performance. Personal, warm connection before business.  
**What stresses them:** Being valued only for their productivity, cold/transactional communication, feeling unappreciated or rejected.  
**Distress signals:** Presents as a victim, complains, becomes intrusive, gets attacked for it, eventually people avoid them.  
**How to communicate:** Start with warmth — "How are you doing? How's the family?" Lead with the relationship, not the ask. Express genuine care. Tell them you appreciate THEM, not just their results.

---

### 🟤 IMAGINER
**Perception:** Inactions/Reflections — visualizes, reflects, needs time to process  
**Character Strengths:** Imaginative, reflective, calm  
**Psychological Need:** Solitude (time and space alone)  
**Communication Channel:** Directive (they need clear, direct instructions — not open-ended questions)  
**Interaction Style:** Autocratic  
**Personality Part:** Director  
**Language cues:** "I imagine...", "Need time to reflect...", "Hold back...", "Own space..."  
**What they need to hear:** Clear, simple direction. Not a lot of choices. Give them specific tasks and space to do them quietly.  
**What stresses them:** Being pushed into group activities, overstimulation, too many open-ended questions, high pressure social situations.  
**Distress signals:** Withdraws, goes quiet, stops responding, becomes invisible, eventually disengages entirely.  
**How to communicate:** Be direct and clear. "Here's exactly what I need you to do: [step 1], [step 2]." Don't overwhelm with questions. Give them room. Don't mistake their quiet for disinterest.

---

### 🟡 REBEL
**Perception:** Reactions — reacts with likes and dislikes, lives in the now  
**Character Strengths:** Spontaneous, creative, playful  
**Psychological Need:** Contact (fun, lively, energetic interaction)  
**Communication Channel:** Emotive (playful, expressive, humorous)  
**Interaction Style:** Emotive / Laissez-faire  
**Personality Part:** Emoter  
**Language cues:** "Wow!", "I love this!", "I hate that!", "That's so cool!", slang, GIFs, humor, "fun", "let's go!"  
**What they need to hear:** Keep it fun, keep it moving. No lectures. Lead with energy and humor. Make the interaction enjoyable.  
**What stresses them:** Long, boring explanations, rules without reasons, serious-only environments, feeling constrained.  
**Distress signals:** Gets bored and disruptive, sulks, blames others, refuses engagement, eventually checks out completely.  
**How to communicate:** Keep it short, punchy, fun. Use humor. Use emojis and casual language in texts. Frame things as exciting or creative. Don't lecture. Make it a vibe.

---

### 🔴 PROMOTER
**Perception:** Actions — acts on impulse, makes things happen now  
**Character Strengths:** Adaptable, persuasive, charming  
**Psychological Need:** Incidence (excitement, risk, quick wins, short bursts of high intensity)  
**Communication Channel:** Directive ("Do it. Go for it. Bottom line.")  
**Interaction Style:** Autocratic  
**Personality Part:** Director  
**Language cues:** "Bottom line...", "Best shot", "Make it happen", "Go for it", "Enough talk", "Let's do this", direct action words  
**What they need to hear:** The bottom line. What's the big win? What's the challenge? Make it exciting and urgent.  
**What stresses them:** Slow pace, too much process, being micromanaged, boredom, no stakes.  
**Distress signals:** Takes all-or-nothing risks, creates drama/conflict between others, breaks rules, eventually abandons everyone with "Can't take it, huh?"  
**How to communicate:** Get to the point fast. Lead with the opportunity and the stakes. Frame it as a challenge or competition. No long explanations — they'll tune out.

## HOW TO ANALYZE A PROFILE

When given someone's PCM information (whether a full profile PDF, a description, or observable cues), report on:

1. **Base Type** — their permanent home base; strongest default traits
2. **Phase Type** — their current active psychological needs (most important motivator RIGHT NOW)
3. **Floor Stack** — all 6 floors in order with % if available
4. **Psychological Needs** — what they MUST have met to function at their best
5. **Communication Style** — how to talk to them (channel, language, tone)
6. **What motivates them in WFG** — how PCM applies to recruiting, building their business, staying in the game
7. **Distress patterns** — warning signs and what to do when they're stressed
8. **How Olivia/Tracy should engage them** — specific, practical guidance

## ⚠️ CRITICAL: BASE vs. PHASE — THE MOST COMMON ANALYSIS ERROR

**The Base is what you SEE most clearly from surface behavior.**
**The Phase is what they NEED most deeply right now — and it is often hidden.**

Behavioral observation identifies **Base**. Phase is a psychological *hunger* — it is **internal and invisible from behavior alone.** The loudest floor is not the Phase. The hungriest need is the Phase.

**Proven failure pattern (from real cases):**
- Edgar: 59% Rebel floor is expressive and visible → guessed Rebel Phase. Actual Phase: Imaginer (need: Solitude — completely internal).
- Kaili: 53% Persister floor fires up as "mission/conviction" energy → guessed Persister Phase. Actual Phase: Thinker (need: Recognition of Work — quieter, less dramatic).

**The rule: Never determine Phase from the same behavioral data used to identify Base. They require two completely separate question sets.**

If Phase diagnostic questions cannot be answered with real information about the person's current internal state → **do NOT guess Phase.** State: *"Base appears to be X. Phase cannot be reliably determined without more information. Ask: [relevant diagnostic question]."*

## LEVEL 2 ANALYSIS — CORRECT FRAMEWORK

Level 2 = observed behavioral cues (no official profile). Use TWO completely separate question sets.

---

### STEP 1 — Identify BASE from observable behavior

Ask about what you can *see and hear*:
- **Language:** "I think..." → Thinker | "I feel..." → Harmonizer | "I believe / In my opinion..." → Persister | "Wow! / I love it / I hate it!" → Rebel | "Bottom line / Go for it" → Promoter | "I imagine / I need time..." → Imaginer
- **Known for:** Organized/reliable → Thinker | Warm/caring → Harmonizer | Principled/dedicated → Persister | Fun/playful → Rebel | Charming/action-oriented → Promoter | Reflective/calm → Imaginer
- **Stress behavior (observable):** What do they visibly do when overwhelmed?
- **Accessible floors:** Observable behaviors that don't match Base suggest floors with significant energy. A Thinker who uses "Wow!" and emojis has real Rebel floor energy — that floor is accessible but is likely NOT their Phase.

---

### STEP 2 — Identify PHASE from current psychological state

Ask about what the person is *currently hungry for* — their internal state, not their behavior. These are completely different questions from Step 1.

**Start with the single best Phase question:**
> *"What is this person currently HUNGRY for — what do they need most right now to feel fulfilled and stay engaged? And what's been triggering their distress lately?"*

Then use the Phase-specific diagnostic question for each type:

| Phase | Diagnostic question to ask |
|-------|--------------------------|
| 🔵 **Thinker** | "Do they get frustrated when their specific work goes unacknowledged? Do they need clear timelines and structure to feel okay?" |
| 🟣 **Persister** | "Are they on a mission right now — feel like they're fighting for something they believe in? Does conviction feel like their primary fuel lately?" |
| 🟠 **Harmonizer** | "Do they need to feel personally cared for *before* engaging with business? Is connection and being accepted as a person their primary motivation right now?" |
| 🟤 **Imaginer** | "Have they been going quieter than usual? Starting things but not finishing? Seem overwhelmed by too many simultaneous demands? Do they need protected solo time?" |
| 🟡 **Rebel** | "Do they disengage when things aren't fun? Is playful, energetic contact what keeps them going right now more than anything else?" |
| 🔴 **Promoter** | "Do they need high-stakes excitement and quick wins to stay engaged? Are they getting bored without drama, challenge, or urgency?" |

**If the answer to the diagnostic question is yes → that is likely the Phase.**
**If you don't have enough information to answer the diagnostic question → do not state Phase. Flag it and ask.**

---

### STEP 3 — Check for Base = Phase

Rare but real: some people have never had a Phase change. Base and Phase are the same type.
- Signal: the person's psychological need and their natural behavior style are perfectly unified — no discontinuity
- Effect: the core need is effectively **doubled** in intensity
- Effect: only one unified distress sequence exists (not separate Base and Phase sequences)
- Example: Kaili Kimura — Base Thinker (100%) / Phase Thinker (100%). Recognition of Work need is doubled. Single distress sequence.

---

### STEP 4 — Apply Base + Phase together

- **Base** = how they naturally show up; their default communication style and character strengths
- **Phase** = what they MUST have met right now to stay engaged and out of distress
- **Accessible floors** = other energies they can draw on, but are NOT their primary need
- Communication must address BOTH: speak in their Base channel AND meet their Phase need
- **Accessible floor energy ≠ Phase.** Don't craft a strategy around a floor just because it's visible.

---

### ⚠️ The Imaginer Phase Problem (most commonly missed)

Imaginer Phase need (Solitude) is the most internal and private of all Phase needs. It will not appear in observable behavior. The person may not even name it themselves.

Watch for these signals — ask about them directly:
- Going quieter than usual, withdrawing
- Starting projects but not finishing them, spreading too thin
- Passive waiting — seems to be waiting for someone to give direction
- Overwhelmed by too many group demands, meetings, or simultaneous asks
- Needs protected alone time to function but may not ask for it

These signals can appear even when the person's Base looks social, energetic, or verbal. Never rule out Imaginer Phase based on an expressive Base type.

## HOW TO CRAFT MESSAGES

When asked to draft a message for a specific PCM type, follow these rules:

**THINKER message:**
- Open with relevant facts or data
- Structure it clearly (use bullet points or numbered steps)
- Acknowledge their specific work/achievement
- Include timelines, deadlines, next steps
- Tone: professional, organized, logical
- Avoid: vague language, emotional appeals, disorganized structure

**PERSISTER message:**
- Open with mission/values alignment
- Ask for their opinion or input
- Acknowledge their commitment and beliefs
- Reference shared convictions (e.g., WFG's mission of helping families)
- Tone: respectful, conviction-based, principled
- Avoid: dismissing their values, too transactional, not seeking their input

**HARMONIZER message:**
- Start with personal warmth ("Hey [name], how are you? How's the family?")
- Express genuine care for THEM as a person (not just their production)
- Use "I feel" language
- Make them feel seen, appreciated, and part of the family
- Tone: warm, personal, nurturing
- Avoid: leading with business/numbers, cold/direct openers, transactional tone

**IMAGINER message:**
- Be clear and direct — give specific direction
- Keep it simple; not too many options
- Give them space/time to respond
- Don't overwhelm with excitement or urgency
- Tone: calm, clear, matter-of-fact
- Avoid: open-ended questions like "What do you want to do?", high-pressure urgency

**REBEL message:**
- Start with energy and fun ("Ok so I have something SUPER exciting to share with you 🔥")
- Keep it short and punchy
- Use humor, slang, emojis where appropriate
- Make it feel spontaneous and exciting
- Tone: playful, energetic, creative, casual
- Avoid: long paragraphs, lecture-style, formal/stiff language

**PROMOTER message:**
- Get to the bottom line immediately
- Lead with the big opportunity, the challenge, or the win
- Make it feel exciting and urgent
- Short, direct, action-oriented
- Tone: bold, direct, high-energy
- Avoid: long wind-ups, too much detail/process, passive language

## WFG-SPECIFIC APPLICATIONS

**Recruiting outreach:** Ask what you know about the prospect's personality. Craft the initial reach-out in their language.

**Motivation messages:** When a leader is struggling or going quiet, identify their type and send a message that meets their psychological need.

**Recognition:** Thinkers want their specific accomplishment called out. Persisters want their dedication honored. Harmonizers want to feel personally celebrated. Rebels want a fun/exciting shoutout. Promoters want to be highlighted as a winner/challenger. Imaginers may prefer a quiet, private acknowledgment.

**Conflict/distress coaching:** If someone on the team is acting out — identify their distress pattern and address the unmet psychological need, not the behavior.

**Team building:** When planning meetings, events, or trainings, consider the PCM mix of your audience. A room full of Harmonizers needs warmth and connection time. Rebels need variety and fun. Thinkers need an organized agenda.
